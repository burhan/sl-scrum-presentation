💽 Software Development

The software development process is a cycle, broadly:

  • Define Requirements
  • Component breakdown
  • Writing software
  • Testing (various kinds)
  • Release Management
  • Bug Fixes

Waterfall Methodology - top down incremental building.

♻ Scrum

An agile process that focuses on delivering the highest business value in the shortest time.

Key Properties of the Scrum Process:

  • Rapid iterations (development cycles). 2-weeks
  • Business sets priorities. Teams organize themselves.
  • Continuous release, continous improvement.
  • Each part of the software development process is executed, but in smaller increments.

The Scrum Process, Highly Simplified:

  • All requirements are captured in a backlog.
  • Series of backlog items are scheduled for a sprint which is a period of work.
  • Estimates make sure that each sprint leads to a deliverable, something to see or test.
  • During the sprint, no changes are accepted. The team is isolated.
  • The team has daily status meeting (called a standup). No discussion in this meeting, only three questions, which everyone answers:
    • What did you do yesterday?
    • What will you do today?
    • Is anything in your way?

ü§∑‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è How Does Scrum Work?

Sprint Review

  • Held at the end of each sprint
  • Team presents what was done during the sprint (usually a demo)
  • No slides, 2-hour preparation time. Informal meeting
  • Entire team participates, the entire world is invited.

📌 Scrum Summary

  • Quick, iterative process. 2-4 week sprints, then a sprint review.
  • Requires discipline from both the owner and the developers.
  • Scrum Master, Product Owner, Team, Customer, (others)

ü§¶ Pitfalls

  • Lack of time discipline and management
  • Vague requirements
  • Interruptions during sprints

A lean method to manage and improve work across human systems. This approach aims to manage work by balancing the demands with available capacity, and improving the handling of system level bottlenecks.
  • Originally developed in Japan by Toyota in the 1940s
  • ÁúãÊùø means "sign board"
  • Just In Time Scheduling

🤔 How Does Kanban Work?

  • A workboard, separated into vertical columns (called swimlanes) representing a stage in the process.
  • A series of cards. Each card represents a work item.
  • Each lane has a limit of how many items it can contain, called the Work In Progress (WIP) limit.
  • The board provides a quick overview of the status of the project.

Typical Kanban Process:

  • Take the top card from the backlog. Place it on the bottom in a swimlane.
  • If the swimlane is full (WIP limit reached), everyone contributes to reduce the number of cards in the swimlane.
  • Cards progress along the swimlane.
  • Anyone is free to work on any card.
  • The product owner will arrange cards in order of importance. Important cards float to the top of the lanes.

📌 Kanban Summary

The Kanban system is simple, but can be used to manage any kind of complicated process.

  • The key limit is the WIP. Each lane has its own WIP.
  • Cards are arranged in order or priority.
  • No limit to the number of swimlanes.
  • Can be used to manage any kind of work; not limited to software development.

üí° Tips, Tools and Techniques

Tips

  • Understand and know your bandwidth.
  • Start with paper, pens, and sticky notes.
  • Understand your project plan. Are there well defined milestones?
  • Hire someone who has done this to coach you through.
  • Ask your development resources which methodolgy they use, and how they manage it.
  • You will need different tools for different stages of development.
